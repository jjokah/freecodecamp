Egusi Soup Recipe

Egusi soup is a delicious and popular West African dish, especially in Nigeria. It's made with ground melon seeds (egusi), vegetables, and a variety of proteins.

Ingredients

  • For the Soup Base:
    • 2 cups egusi (melon seeds)(ground)
    • 1 cup palm oil
    • 1 large onion (chopped)
    • 3 cups meat or fish stock (or water)
    • 1 teaspoon ground crayfish (optional)
    • 2 tablespoons ground pepper (adjust to taste)
    • 2 stock cubes
    • Salt (to taste)
  • For the Proteins:
    • 500g beef, goat meat, or chicken
    • 200g assorted meats (shaki, pomo, etc.)
    • 200g smoked/dried fish (washed and deboned)
  • For the vegetables:
    • 2 cups ugwu (fluted pumpkin leaves) or spinach
    • 1 cup bitter leaf (optional, washed to reduce bitterness)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Meat & Fish
    1. Wash and season the meat with salt, seasoning cubes, and onions.
    2. Boil until tender, then set aside.
    3. Add the dried/smoked fish to the meat stock
  2. Cook the Egusi
    1. Heat the palm oil in a pot over medium heat.
    2. Add chopped onions and fry for about 2 minutes.
    3. Stir in the ground egusi and fry for about 5-7 minutes, stirring constantly.
  3. Make the Soup Base
    1. Pour in the meat stock gradually and mix well. Let it simmer for 10 minutes.
    2. Add the crayfish, pepper, and seasoning cubes. Stir and allow to cook for 5 minutes.
  4. Add the Proteins & Vegetables
    1. Add the cooked meat and fish to the pot, stir, and simmer for another 5 minutes.
    2. Finally, add the vegetables, stir, and let it cook for 3-5 minutes.
  5. Serve and Enjoy!
    1. Adjust salt and seasoning as needed, then remove from heat.
    2. Serve hot with pounded yam, fufu, or eba
