Egusi Soup Recipe

Egusi soup is a delicious and popular West African dish, especially in Nigeria. It's made with ground melon seeds (egusi), vegetables, and a variety of proteins, creating a rich and hearty meal.

Ingredients

  • For the Soup Base:
    • 2 cups egusi (melon seeds), ground
    • 1 cup palm oil
    • 1 large onion, chopped
    • 3 cups meat or fish stock (or water)
    • 1 teaspoon ground crayfish (optional)
    • 2 tablespoons ground pepper (adjust to taste)
    • 2 stock cubes
    • Salt (to taste)
  • For the Proteins:
    • 500g beef, goat meat, or chicken
    • 200g assorted meats (shaki, pomo, etc.)
    • 200g smoked/dried fish (washed and deboned)
  • For the Vegetables:
    • 2 cups ugwu (fluted pumpkin leaves) or spinach
    • 1 cup bitter leaf (optional, washed to reduce bitterness)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Meat & Fish
    1. Wash and season the meat with salt, seasoning cubes, and onions.
    2. Boil until tender, then set aside the meat and reserve the stock.
    3. Wash the dried/smoked fish thoroughly, debone if necessary, and add it to the reserved meat stock to soften slightly.
  2. Cook the Egusi
    1. Heat the palm oil in a clean, dry pot over medium heat until hot but not smoking.
    2. Add the chopped onions and fry until fragrant and translucent (about 2-3 minutes).
    3. Lower the heat, add the ground egusi, and fry for about 5-10 minutes, stirring constantly to prevent burning. It should look crumbly. (Alternatively, mix egusi with a little water into a paste before adding).
  3. Make the Soup Base
    1. Gradually pour the meat/fish stock into the pot with the fried egusi, stirring continuously to combine well and avoid lumps.
    2. Add the ground crayfish (if using), ground pepper, and crumbled stock cubes. Stir well, cover the pot, and let it simmer for about 10-15 minutes, allowing the egusi to cook through and the flavors to meld. Stir occasionally.
  4. Add the Proteins & Vegetables
    1. Add the cooked meat, assorted meats, and softened fish to the simmering egusi soup. Stir gently to combine. Let it simmer for another 5-10 minutes so the proteins absorb the soup's flavor.
    2. Stir in the washed vegetables (Ugwu/Spinach and/or Bitter leaf). Cook for just 3-5 minutes until the leaves are wilted but still retain some freshness and color. Do not overcook the vegetables.
  5. Serve and Enjoy!
    1. Taste the soup and adjust salt and seasoning if necessary. Once satisfied, remove the pot from the heat.
    2. Serve hot with your favorite swallow, such as Pounded Yam, Fufu (Cassava or Semolina), Eba (Garri), or Amala.
